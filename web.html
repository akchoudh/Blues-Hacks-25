Indicators

Canada-US Exchange rate

An exchange rate is the rate at which one currency can be exchanged for another between nations or economic zones. It is used to determine the value of various currencies in relation to each other and is important in determining trade and capital flow dynamics.

Unemployment Rate

The unemployment rate provides an indication of the health of our economy. A higher unemployment rate can mean the region is contributing less to the economy in terms of the goods or services that are being produced.

Consumer Price Index

The CPI measures inflation as experienced by consumers in their day-to-day living expenses.

Housing

Housing is becoming more and more important in Canada

Interest rates

Interest rates are an indicator of overall economic health. When rates are low, it can stimulate borrowing and spending, benefiting your financial situation. High rates can slow economic growth and lead to job market challenges. However they can be used too combat challenges faced by an economy

Export Value

Export values are the current value of exports converted to U.S. dollars and expressed as a percentage of the average for the base period
